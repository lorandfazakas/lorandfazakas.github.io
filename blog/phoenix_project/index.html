Book recommendation: The Phoenix Project

March 7, 2020 Lorand Fazakas

2 minutes read

 Twitter

 Facebook

 Reddit

 LinkedIn

IT operations isn’t always a fun place to work at. Murphy’s law says that "whatever can go wrong, will go wrong". Servers will crash, applications will freeze and security vulnerabilities are everywhere.

About DevOps

DevOps is a movement to bring teams together and work as one for the same goal. Build, test, ship and run applications multiple times a day, introducing only small changes at a time and automating the whole delivery process (CI/CD).

A novel about DevOps

The Phoenix Project will introduce you to a company in crisis because of the Dev, QA and IT operations teams can’t get on the same page. Every team focuses on their respective jobs but ignores the rest and this mindset will result in a toxic environment.

The book was written by Gene Kim alongside Kevin Behr and George Spafford. It’s a story about an IT manager who finds himself promoted into a lead role at a fictional American company ‘Parts Unlimited’. Bill has 90 days to fix so far disastrous initiative called the The Phoenix Project or the IT department will be sourced out. Bill will find himself under constant pressure and this will affect his personal life as well. Bill’s journey is to figure out how to improve communication and effectivenes across the whole organization. It’s also interesting to note how analogous IT is to more traditional processes like manufacturing products.

Three-way-thinking

The book will introduce you to a concept called the three-way-thinking.

  • Systems thinking: look at the system’s perfomance as a whole
  • Amplify Feedback Loops
  • Culture of continous experimentation and learning

Conclusion

This is a must read for any IT professional or manager who works at a medium to large organisation. The situations in this book will resemble with the tasks you will face at your work every day.

Finally, its a great read about methodologies of DevOps, Lean, Agile and Theory of Constraints.
